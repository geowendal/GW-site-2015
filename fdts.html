FINANCIAL DISCLOSURE TRACKING SYSTEM

What I did: Task Analysis, Information Architecture, Interaction Design, User Research

This project involved developing a administrative system for the Office of Government Ethics that would support the work performed by the staff - tracking financial disclosure information for government employees. Background work involved studying the current system, and conducting interviews with the staff to better understand their work and identifying pain points with the current system. After completing the interviews, I mapped out the workflows for all the different types of tasks performed within the office. These workflows were also used to identify which tasks would be performed within the system, and which fell outside its scope.

The application had a complex set of requirements, including tracking financial information for the same individual across various positions and agencies and the ability to generate ad hoc reports containing records meeting conditional user-defined criteria. After some early sketching, the first iterations of the design were developed as basic wireframes.

I added some basic interactivity to the wireframes, which allowed me to conduct usability testing before the design had gotten too far along. This early feedback was extremely valuable in finding ways to optimize the usability of the application.

After completing the wireframes, the design was handed off for a visual treatment designed to match OGE's website (www.oge.gov). I consulted on the visual design (but was not responsible for it myself), and after approval the application moved into production and was successfully implemented.
